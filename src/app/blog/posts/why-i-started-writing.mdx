I've spent years learning things online.

Some of it was useful immediately. Some of it wasn't.

I've gone down rabbit holes in software development, machine learning, product design, entrepreneurship, music production, video editing and plenty of other topics that caught my attention at the time.

What I've realized is that a lot of what I learn ends up staying in my head. I move on to the next project, the next idea, or the next challenge without taking the time to document what I've learned along the way.

This blog is my attempt to change that.

I want a place where I can share things I'm building, lessons I'm learning, mistakes I'm making, and thoughts that I think are worth putting into words.

Some posts will be about technology.

Some will be about startups and product development.

Some might just be observations from projects I'm working on.

I don't claim to have all the answers. In fact, one thing I've learned from building things is how often you're wrong on the first attempt.

That's part of the process.

Right now, a lot of my time is going into building For You Commerce, a platform focused on making social media shopping safer and more trustworthy. Working on it has already taught me lessons about products, people, business, and problem-solving that I probably wouldn't have learned anywhere else.

I'm sure many of those experiences will find their way into future posts.

For now, this is simply a starting point.

No grand announcement.

No promise that every post will be groundbreaking.

Just a place to think out loud, document the journey, and hopefully share something useful along the way.

Welcome.
